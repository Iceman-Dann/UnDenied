Why This Exists

Every year, millions of people receive letters written by lawyers whose job is to make them give up. Insurance companies spend millions refining the exact language that is most likely to discourage an appeal. Government agencies produce denial notices in bureaucratic language so dense it takes a law degree to parse. Landlords send notices with intentional omissions, counting on tenants not knowing what's missing. The letters work. Most people don't fight back. That is by design.

The moment this became real: a grandmother who lost her Medicare coverage for a mobility device she'd had for eleven years. The denial letter was three pages. The appeal deadline was buried in page two, paragraph four, in a font two sizes smaller than everything else. She didn't appeal. She didn't know she could. She didn't know she would have won. A family in Cleveland received an eviction notice on a Friday. They had 72 hours under state law to respond in writing. They didn't know. They left. The landlord was later found to have served an invalid notice. They had a winning case they never filed.

I am a student. I don't have a law degree, and I don't work for a firm. What I have is a Gemini API key, 20 days, and the conviction that no one should lose a fight simply because a letter was written to confuse them. This is what I built instead of sleeping.
